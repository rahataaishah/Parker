Case Study: Property for an Auto Shop

  • Problem
  • Solution

Business was flourishing for Jose and his auto body shop in California, but the absence of a proper parking lot was hindering his ability to keep up with the rising demand. Half of the 5,000 sq. ft shop he rented was being utilized for car storage, while the other half was dedicated to repairs. Recognizing the need to expand, Jose began searching for a larger building.

He came across a property for sale that offered a spacious 6,000 sq. ft workspace along with 5,000 sq. ft of parking space, equivalent to around 20 parking spots. In addition to providing ample room for his operations, the mortgage payment for this property would be only half of what he was paying in rent. The listed price for the property was $350,000, making it an appealing prospect for Jose.

Initially, Jose approached a loan officer at another SBA lender for a commercial real estate loan. However, he faced a hurdle when he discovered that the lender's minimum loan amount for commercial real estate was $650,000. Given that this would be his first business loan, Jose was hesitant about taking on such a substantial debt.

Fortunately, the loan officer at the competing loan provider set aside any rivalry and recommended Jose to their institution. Once Jose contacted his loan officer at Parker Financial Services, everything started falling into place. Within just 60 days of initiating the loan application, Jose found himself the proud owner of his new building.

Parker Financial Services was able to provide 90% financing, taking into account Jose's strong industry experience and the fact that the loan payment would be lower than his previous rent. With the move to the new building, Jose wasted no time in hiring two additional technicians to tackle the backlog of jobs that had accumulated over the past month.

Moreover, the lower mortgage payment resulting from the loan proved to be a game-changer for Jose's business during the unforeseen arrival of the COVID-19 pandemic in March 2020. While three out of five competing auto body shops in the area closed due to the pandemic, Jose's business thrived with reduced competition and the ability to maintain "business as usual."

Thanks to the support and financial relief provided by Parker Financial Services, Jose's auto body shop continues to prosper. The expanded facilities, increased staff, and affordable financing have played crucial roles in driving the success of his business.

Case Study: Insurance Coverage for Gas Station

  • Problem
  • Solution

Rajiv, the owner of a small gas station operating in California, found himself facing a substantial challenge when it came to securing comprehensive insurance coverage to safeguard his business from potential risks. Located in an area known for its seismic activity and vulnerability to earthquakes, Rajiv's concerns grew as he contemplated the potential financial implications of property damage, business interruptions, and liability claims arising from natural disasters and accidents. Realizing the shortcomings of his existing insurance policy, which provided inadequate coverage for such risks, Rajiv became acutely aware of the immense vulnerability his gas station faced. Without adequate protection, the potential for substantial financial losses loomed, posing a threat that could potentially cripple his business and cast a shadow of uncertainty over its long-term viability. Rajiv recognized the pressing need for a comprehensive insurance solution tailored to his unique circumstances, one that would mitigate his exposure to risks and provide the necessary financial safety net to ensure the continuity and resilience of his gas station in the face of unforeseen events.

Recognizing the unique risks faced by Rajiv's gas station, Parker Financial Studies stepped in to analyze his insurance needs and devise a tailored solution. Through a meticulous assessment, they recommended a comprehensive insurance package that encompassed multiple key components to adequately protect Rajiv's business. Firstly, they advised Rajiv to obtain property insurance that specifically covered damages caused by earthquakes and other perils prevalent in the region. This coverage would provide the financial resources needed to repair or rebuild the gas station and replace damaged equipment and inventory in the aftermath of an earthquake. Secondly, Parker Financial Studies proposed business interruption insurance to mitigate the financial consequences of potential disruptions. This coverage would compensate Rajiv for lost income during periods when the gas station is temporarily closed due to earthquake-related damages or necessary repairs. Lastly, recognizing the importance of liability protection, they recommended liability insurance that would safeguard Rajiv's business against third-party claims arising from accidents or injuries on the premises. This coverage would cover legal expenses, medical costs, and potential settlements or judgments, protecting Rajiv from significant financial liabilities that could otherwise jeopardize the future of his gas station. By implementing this comprehensive insurance solution, Rajiv could rest assured that his business was adequately protected against the potential risks associated with operating in an earthquake-prone area. The expertise and tailored approach provided by Parker Financial Studies ensured that Rajiv's gas station could continue to operate with confidence and resilience, even in the face of unforeseen events.
